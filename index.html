Music.

Music is a form of art and cultural activity whose medium is sound organised in time. Common musical elements include pitch(governor of melody and harmony), rythm(and its associated concepts tempo, meter and articulation), dynamics(including softness and loudness)together with the sonic qualities of timbre and texture.

Musical is performed over a wide range of in instruments and vocal techniques examples including singing, rapping. In general form activities describing music alo include the production and creation of musical works.


History.

Pre-historic music can only be theorized based on findings from paleolithic archeological sites. The Divje Babe flute, carved from a cave bear femur is thought to be at least 40000 thousand years old.

The ancient egyptians credited one of their gods(thoth) with the invention of music. The earliest material and evidence of egyptian musical instruments dates to the predynastic period, but the evidence is more securely attested in the old kingdom when harps, flutes and double clarinets were played.

The different periods of music.

There later on came different periods of music such as renaissance, baroque, classical, romantic and 20th and 21st century music.

Renaissance.

This music group is ranged to have had a lifespan of around 1400-1600AD. There existed different themes such as courtly love. The music during this period was considered to be secular. Examples of producers Thomas Morley.

Baroque.

The baroque era of music took place from 1600-1750AD. This style flourished accros Europe. During this time, music expanded in its range and complexity. German baroque composers wrote for small ensambles including strings, brass and woodwinds. There were also choirs and keyboard instruments such as pipe organs, harpischords and others.

Classical.

This period lasted from 1730-1820AD. It aimed to imitate what were seen as the key elements of the art and philosophy of ancient Greece and Rome. It had a lighter, simpler texture and clearer than baroque music. Examples include Joseph Haydn.

20th and 21st century music.

With the 20th century came the rise in electronic music technology. Radio broadcasting and the mass market availability ment that sound recordings of songs and pieces heard by listeners became the main way to learn about new songs and pieces. Jazz in this period became an important genre of music over the course of the 20th century. Rock music also developed in the 1960s and 1950s became a popular genre of musc.


Sociological aspect of music.

Many ethnographic studies demonstrate that music is a community based activity. Music is experieced by individuals in a range of social setting ranging from being alone to attending a large concert, in the end forming a music community. Other types of music such as jazz, blues, country and many other types are often performed in clubs and theatres. This types enabled the audience to drink and dance and express their satisfaction by cheering. It was in Europe and North America where there was often a divide between what types of music was viewed as low culture and high culture.

Media and technology in music.

Long ago, music could only be listened to live, in an outdoor or indoor space, concert hall, cabarnet room or a theatre. Since the 20th century, live music can also be broadcasted over the radio, television, internet or recorded and listened to on a CD player or mp3. Some music styles focus on producing a sound for a performance while others on recordings to be listened through from a medium.
